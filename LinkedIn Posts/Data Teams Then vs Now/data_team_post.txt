𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮 𝘁𝗲𝗮𝗺𝘀 𝗹𝗼𝗼𝗸 𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘆 𝗱𝗶𝗳𝗳𝗲𝗿𝗲𝗻𝘁 𝘁𝗵𝗮𝗻 𝘁𝗵𝗲𝘆 𝗱𝗶𝗱 𝟱 𝘆𝗲𝗮𝗿𝘀 𝗮𝗴𝗼.

The old model: 10-15 person teams. A VP at the top. Layers of managers. Data engineers, analysts, BI developers—all siloed. You'd submit a ticket and wait weeks for a dashboard.

It worked. But it was slow, expensive, and built for a world where humans were the only consumers of data.

That world is gone.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗻𝗲𝘄 𝗺𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹 𝗹𝗼𝗼𝗸𝘀 𝗺𝗼𝗿𝗲 𝗹𝗶𝗸𝗲 𝘁𝗵𝗶𝘀:

→ 1-3 people, not 15 
→ A strategic leader who's hands-on, not just managing 
→ Direct partnership with business stakeholders 
→ Self-service analytics so teams aren't waiting on reports 
→ Data infrastructure built for AI agents, not just dashboards

The companies getting this right aren't building bigger data teams. They're building leaner, smarter ones—and filling gaps with fractional expertise when they need it.

Why? Because AI is changing what data teams actually do. Less time building reports. More time building the foundations that let AI do the heavy lifting.

The question isn't "how many data people do we need?"

It's "do we have the right data infrastructure to let a small team—and AI—do the work of many?"

That's the shift. And it's happening fast.
