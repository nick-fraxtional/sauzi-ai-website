𝗠𝗢𝗡𝗗𝗔𝗬 𝗠𝗘𝗧𝗥𝗜𝗖𝗦: 𝗖𝗟𝗧𝗩
Customer Lifetime Value might be the most important metric most companies get wrong. Not because the math is hard. But because the data behind it is a mess.

𝗪𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘁 𝗶𝘀: 
CLTV is the total revenue you can expect from a single customer over the entire relationship. It's the foundation for answering questions like:
→ How much can we spend to acquire a customer?
→ Which segments deserve more investment?
→ Is our business actually healthy long-term?

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗯𝗮𝘀𝗶𝗰 𝗳𝗼𝗿𝗺𝘂𝗹𝗮: 
CLTV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan

Simple enough. But here's where it falls apart:
→𝗔𝘃𝗲𝗿𝗮𝗴𝗲 𝗢𝗿𝗱𝗲𝗿 𝗩𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗲 Are you pulling gross or net revenue? Are refunds accounted for? Discounts? If finance and marketing are using different numbers, your AOV is already wrong.
→𝗣𝘂𝗿𝗰𝗵𝗮𝘀𝗲 𝗙𝗿𝗲𝗾𝘂𝗲𝗻𝗰𝘆 This requires clean customer identification. If the same customer exists as three different records in your database—congratulations, you just undercounted frequency by 3x.
→𝗖𝘂𝘀𝘁𝗼𝗺𝗲𝗿 𝗟𝗶𝗳𝗲𝘀𝗽𝗮𝗻 When does a customer "start"? First purchase? Account creation? When do they "end"? Last activity? Formal churn? If you don't have clear definitions, your lifespan calculation is a guess.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗿𝗲𝗮𝗹 𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗯𝗹𝗲𝗺: Most companies don't have a CLTV problem. They have a data problem dressed up as a CLTV problem. They're making million-dollar decisions on acquisition spend, retention programs, and customer segmentation based on a number that's built on inconsistent definitions, duplicated records, and mismatched data sources.

𝗛𝗼𝘄 𝘁𝗼 𝗳𝗶𝘅 𝗶𝘁: Before you trust your CLTV, audit the inputs.
✓ Do you have a single, deduplicated customer ID?
✓ Is revenue tracked consistently across systems?
✓ Are your definitions of "active" and "churned" documented and agreed upon?
✓ Can you trace the calculation back to source data?

If you can't confidently answer yes to all four, your CLTV isn't a metric—it's a hope.
