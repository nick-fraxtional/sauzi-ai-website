Monday Metrics: CLTV

Why Customer Lifetime Value might be the most important metric most companies get wrong.

Customer Lifetime Value might be the most important metric most companies get wrong. Not because the math is hard. But because the data behind it is a mess.

What it is:

CLTV is the total revenue you can expect from a single customer over the entire relationship. It's the foundation for answering questions like:

  • How much can we spend to acquire a customer?
  • Which segments deserve more investment?
  • Is our business actually healthy long-term?

The basic formula:

CLTV = Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan

Simple enough. But here's where it falls apart:

  • Average Order Value: Are you pulling gross or net revenue? Are refunds accounted for? Discounts? If finance and marketing are using different numbers, your AOV is already wrong.
  • Purchase Frequency: This requires clean customer identification. If the same customer exists as three different records in your database—congratulations, you just undercounted frequency by 3x.
  • Customer Lifespan: When does a customer "start"? First purchase? Account creation? When do they "end"? Last activity? Formal churn? If you don't have clear definitions, your lifespan calculation is a guess.

The real problem

Most companies don't have a CLTV problem. They have a data problem dressed up as a CLTV problem. They're making million-dollar decisions on acquisition spend, retention programs, and customer segmentation based on a number that's built on inconsistent definitions, duplicated records, and mismatched data sources.

How to fix it

Before you trust your CLTV, audit the inputs.

  • Do you have a single, deduplicated customer ID?
  • Is revenue tracked consistently across systems?
  • Are your definitions of "active" and "churned" documented and agreed upon?
  • Can you trace the calculation back to source data?

If you can't confidently answer yes to all four, your CLTV isn't a metric—it's a hope.
